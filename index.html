Coca-Cola Rice Recipe

History

The origin of the recipe is quite uncertain. But the place where it is popular (the Colombian Caribbean) may tell us where it comes from. First, the availability of ingredients, coconuts close to the beach, rice from warm-land crops and well… Coca-Cola is everywhere. About this, I doubt it was the original ingredient. Probably, it came as a replacement of a sugar cane derived product called “panela”. Second, a cultural feature of having rice as a side of almost any dish. Third, a little bit of the Arabic influence that the region has had throughout years. The combination of sweat and salt components in meals is a prominent characteristic of Arabic cuisine (think of almond rice). Thus, probably as part of the inherited taste, this recipe appeared.

Ingredients

You will cook for 4-6 people during 45 minutes with:

  1. Two cups of long-grain rice
  2. One can of coconout milk
  3. Three cups of regular Coca-Cola
  4. One cup of water
  5. Salt
  6. Half of a medium-size onion
  7. Two spoons of any vegetable oil
  8. Optional: raisins (if you want them softer, soak them in a cup of coffee for a while)

Back

Preparation

  1. In a wide pan at high-medium heat, pour the whole can of coconut milk. Let the water to evaporate, so what is left in the pan is the oil and traces of coconut. This is called “titoté”. Stir it, so it gets a caramel-like colour without burning it. Be patient, it takes a while.
  2. With high heat, add the rice and stir it with the “titoté” during one minute. Add the Coca-Cola, water, salt, oil and raisins. All the solid ingredients will be covered under the liquids.
  3. Leave the high heat until you can see the rice and raisins in the surface. Now, settle a low temperature and cover the rice. Seldomly, check the pan and stir the rice, so it does not get stuck to the bottom.
  4. Once there is no more water or liquids left serve it warm. Enjoy!
